About This Project

Understanding Clause-Level Classification and Entity Extraction in Legal Contracts

What Does This Project Do?

This project uses Natural Language Processing techniques to automatically analyze legal contract documents. It processes uploaded contracts to segment them into meaningful clauses and extract legally relevant information, enabling users to quickly identify important provisions and entities such as parties, dates, obligations, and references without manually reviewing lengthy documents.

Instead of spending hours manually reading through contracts or case files, this system does the work in seconds and presents everything in an organized, easy-to-read format.

How Does It Work?

The system works in three simple steps:

  1. Upload: You upload a PDF document containing legal text (contracts, case files, legal briefs, etc.)
  2. Analyze & Structure: The system first segments the contract into individual clauses and classifies them based on their legal function. It then extracts legally relevant entities from each identified clause and organizes the results into structured categories such as:
    • Clause Categories: Contractual provisions such as confidentiality, termination, payment, liability, and dispute resolution
    • Contracting Parties: Names of individuals or organizations involved in the agreement
    • Dates & Durations: Effective dates, expiration periods, notice timelines, and deadlines
    • Financial Terms: Payment amounts, penalties, fees, and other monetary obligations
    • Legal References: Governing law clauses, statutory mentions, and jurisdictional references
    • Rights & Obligations: Duties, restrictions, and entitlements defined within contractual clauses

    • Compliance: Track regulatory requirements and identify compliance-related information

    The Technology Behind It

    This system is built using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques for analyzing legal contract text. It leverages a LegalBERT-based machine learning model, trained on legal documents, to identify clause structures, classify contractual provisions, and extract legally relevant entities based on contextual patterns in the text.

    Instead of relying on manual keyword searches, the system analyzes the linguistic and structural characteristics of legal language, enabling it to interpret contractual content in a way that supports accurate clause-level analysis and information extraction.
